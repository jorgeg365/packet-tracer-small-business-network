Main router design notes

Interfaces

- GigabitEthernet0/0: trunk to switch
- GigabitEthernet0/0.10: VLAN 10, 192.168.10.1/24
- GigabitEthernet0/0.20: VLAN 20, 192.168.20.1/24
- GigabitEthernet0/0.30: VLAN 30, 192.168.30.1/24
- GigabitEthernet0/0.40: VLAN 40, 192.168.40.1/24
- GigabitEthernet0/0.50: VLAN 50, 192.168.50.1/24
- GigabitEthernet0/1: NAT outside, 200.1.1.2/24

NAT

- Inside interfaces: the VLAN subinterfaces on GigabitEthernet0/0
- Outside interface: GigabitEthernet0/1
- PAT/overload enabled for the internal 192.168.0.0/16 space

DHCP relay

- Helper address: 192.168.10.2 on VLANs 20, 30, 40, and 50

ACL policy

- HR VLAN is restricted from IT VLAN
- Guest VLAN is intended to stay isolated from internal networks
